Gmail "Send As" Feature

A comprehensive guide to setting up and using multiple email identities in Gmail

What is Gmail's "Send As" Feature?

Gmail's "Send As" feature allows you to send emails from different email addresses using your primary Gmail account. This is perfect for managing multiple email identities without having to log in and out of different accounts.

Setting Up "Send As" in Gmail

Follow these simple steps to configure additional email addresses in your Gmail account:

1

Access Gmail Settings

Click the gear icon in the top right corner of your Gmail interface, then select "See all settings".

2

Navigate to Accounts Tab

Go to the "Accounts and Import" tab in the settings menu.

3

Add Another Email Address

In the "Send mail as" section, click on "Add another email address".

4

Enter Email Details

Enter your name and the email address you want to add. You can also choose to treat it as an alias.

5

Configure SMTP Settings

Enter the SMTP server information for the email provider of your additional address. For example, if adding a Yahoo email, you would use Yahoo's SMTP settings.

6

Verify Email Address

Gmail will send a verification code to the email address you're adding. Check that email and enter the verification code to complete the setup.

Using the "Send As" Feature

Now that you've set up additional email addresses, here's how to use them:

Composing New Emails

When composing a new email, click on the "From" dropdown menu to select which email address you want to send from.

If you don't see the "From" field, click on the "From" link next to the "To" field to display it.

Replying to Emails

When replying to an email, Gmail will automatically use the address that received the original email.

You can still change the "From" address by clicking on the dropdown menu in the reply window.

Setting a Default "Send As" Address

You can set any of your added email addresses as the default "From" address for new emails.

Go back to Settings > Accounts and Import > "Send mail as" section, and click "make default" next to the desired email address.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use any email address with "Send As"?

+

You can use any email address that you own and have access to. You'll need to verify ownership of the email address during setup, and you may need the SMTP server information for that email provider.

Will recipients know I'm using Gmail?

+

By default, Gmail adds a "via gmail.com" tag to emails sent using the "Send As" feature. However, if you configure the SMTP settings for your other email address (rather than using Gmail's servers), this tag won't appear.

Is there a limit to how many email addresses I can add?

+

Gmail allows you to add up to 30 additional email addresses to your account using the "Send As" feature.

Can I use "Send As" with my work email?

+

Yes, as long as your work email provider allows SMTP access. Some organizations may have security policies that prevent this, so check with your IT department first.

What's the difference between "Send As" and "Send mail as"?

+

They refer to the same feature. "Send mail as" is the official name in Gmail settings, while "Send As" is a common shorthand term for the feature.
